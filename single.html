TOP PLACES IN NORTH VIETNAM

1. Ha Noi

Hanoi, the capital and second-largest city of Vietnam, is known as the "city between the rivers." One of the most fascinating cities in Southeast Asia is comprised of the Old Town, the French Quarter, old temples dating back thousands of years, and numerous gorgeous lakes and landmarks. The lively Vietnamese culture is further enhanced by a blend of Chinese and French influences.

2. Moc Chau

Moc Chau is located in Son La Province, roughly 180 kilometers northwest of Hanoi. This mountainous area provides an amazing natural beauty and a refreshing climate due to its location surrounded by massive mountains. Moc Chau, Vietnam, is well-known for its expansive tea plantations, where guests may stroll through verdant fields, observe the tea-making process, and savor the aromas of freshly brewed tea.

How to get there ? Top things

3. Sapa

Sapa is located near the Chinese border in the northwest of the nation. The Hoang Lien Son mountain range, which includes Fansipan, the highest peak in Indochina, surrounds it. The four distinct seasons that make up Sapa's weather each have their own special appeal. Winter is dry and cold with sporadic snowfall, while summer is warm and humid. The ideal seasons to visit Sapa are fall and spring.

How to get there ? Top things

4. Ninh Binh

About ninety kilometers south of Hanoi, in the northern part of the country, is the province of Ninh Binh. It's well-known for its breathtaking scenery, extensive history, and distinctive cultural legacy.

Apart from its scenic splendor, Ninh Binh boasts an extensive past that spans centuries. Several historical treasures and structures from the Dinh and early Le dynasties, when it was the capital of Vietnam, are still visible today.

How to get there ? Top things

5. Ha Giang

Ha Giang is a border province and an official Frontier Area located in the far north of the nation. Traveling to this region is like taking a trip back in time, where you will see some of the most untamed and breathtaking scenery in Vietnam. The ideal way to enjoy Ha Giang is on a two-wheeled road tour where you can take in the magnificence of the scenery and the local flavor of the outlying towns and minority villages.

How to get there ? Top things

6. Thua Thien Hue

Hue can be your long-awaited haven if you have a strong interest in Vietnamese culture or if you're just seeking a break from the bright lights and congestion of big cities. The ancient royal city enchants tourists with beautiful landscapes, a serene environment, religious traditions, hundreds-year-old historic architecture, and key highlights of UNESCO-recognized tangible and intangible cultural heritages—all in spite of Southeast Asia's fast urbanization.

How to get there ? Top things

7. Da Nang

It's no surprise that Da Nang locals have some of the widest smiles in the nation—the city boasts wide beaches, delicious street cuisine, the famed Hai Van Pass, and an expanding array of cafes, restaurants, and bars. My Khe Beach's soft sands stretch southward from the Son Tra Peninsula's mountains. Some of Vietnam's most opulent resorts are located in Da Nang, yet the majority of the beach is still owned by the locals.

How to get there ? Top things

8. Quang Nam

Quang Nam has undergone ups and downs throughout the years, but it has managed to preserve strong human values as well as the distinctive historical and cultural remnants of the past. The Cham Towers in Khuong My, My Son Holy Land, Tra Kieu Old Champa Capital, Hoi An Ancient Town, and Chien Dan, which document the remnants of Sa Huynh, Champa, and the Dai Viet civilization, are a few examples of typical cultural places.

How to get there ? Top things

9. Khanh Hoa

South Central Vietnam is home to the province of Khanh Hoa. It has 200 kilometers of coastline and is home to Nha Trang, a well-known beach resort city that is also the province's capital. Beyond its many beaches, Khanh Hoa is known for its nearly 200 stunning islands, which contribute to the province's well-earned status as a country promised by sun and sand.

How to get there ? Top things

10. Ho Chi Minh

Prospering in creativity, vitality, and traffic—lots of traffic— Formerly known as Saigon, Ho Chi Minh City serves as both the southern region's principal hub and Vietnam's economic center. The dynamic cityscape of HCMC, a free-spirited, multicultural metropolis, combines old and new Vietnam in the smallest possible area, symbolizing both the city's past and future. Ben Thanh Market, Notre Dame Cathedral Basilica, and the War Museum are just a few of this city's well-known attractions.

How to get there ? Top things

11. Can Tho

Can Tho, also referred to as "a river delta city," is renowned for its distinctive native food, highly valued culture, and poetically beautiful rivers. Can Tho is the ideal location if you want to get away from the bustle of the city and spend some time in nature.

How to get there ? Top things

12. Tay Ninh

Over the past few decades, Tay Ninh, Vietnam has gained popularity as a travel destination among both domestic and foreign visitors. With its varied cuisine, hospitable residents, first-rate services, and breathtaking natural beauties, the province is seen as a rising star in the Southeast when it comes to tourism. Many tourists have found themselves enthralled with this country because it is so spiritual, mysterious, adventurous, and full of intrigue.

How to get there ? Top things

13. Vung Tau

The Vietnamese equivalent of fish and chips and a few days by the sea is the charming beach town of Vung Tau. To put it briefly, it's a place where the residents go to unwind, enjoy the sea air, and eat an abundance of seafood. Although Vung Tau lacks Con Dao's idyllic white sand beaches, it's still a great place for a fast trip, only two hours from Ho Chi Minh City. This is the ideal vacation guide for Vung Tau.

How to get there ? Top things

14. Binh Thuan

For local tourists, Binh Thuan has long been somewhat of a best-kept secret. But more tourists looking for one-of-a-kind natural experiences are drawn here by the area's scenic splendor and varied geography. Imagine breathtaking lakes. gurgling waterways. Enchanted, arid areas. rocky shorelines and blustery oceans They're all in Binh Thuan. Because Binh Thuan was formerly a part of the Cham principality of Panduranga, it is also important archaeologically.

How to get there ? Top things

15. Phu Quoc

Though Phu Quoc is known as "Pearl Island" because to its stunning white sand beaches, equal attention should be paid to the island's cultural legacy and environmental preservation initiatives. The main industries on the sleepy island are still fishing and agriculture, and since 2006, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve has covered more than half of it.

How to get there ? Top things

16. Con Dao

Con Dao is a little archipelago off the southern coast where very few travelers arrive. Even though the route takes more work, those who persevere are rewarded with some of the most serene beaches and excellent snorkeling in the nation. There is Con Dao National Park covering a large portion of the island. You will have plenty of opportunities to find your own paradise within its dense jungle.

How to get there ? Top things

17. Hon Son

Southern Vietnam's newest undiscovered beach attraction is Hon Son Island, which is situated in the province of Kien Giang. Hon Son resembles a shining jewel with its pure beauty and graceful, magnificent beaches reflecting the enormous ocean. There are coconut groves along the beach, giving the area a wild appearance, and there are no signs of mining.

How to get there ? Top things

18. Nam Du

Do you desire a tan on your skin? While the islands of Phu Quoc, Ly Son, Ha Long Bay, Co To, and so on might not seem all that weird, Nam Du Island is still relatively unknown to most people. Travel enthusiasts will be astonished by this undiscovered gem in Kien Giang Province's smooth white sand beach, cool green beaches, lounging coconut shade, and pristine sceneries.
